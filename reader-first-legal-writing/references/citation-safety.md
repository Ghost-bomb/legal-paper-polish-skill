# Citation Safety

Protect legal substance before improving expression.

## Sensitive Content

Treat these as high-risk:

- footnote-supported propositions;
- direct quotations;
- statutes, treaties, judicial interpretations, administrative rules, and normative documents;
- case facts and holdings;
- data, years, names, institutions, and technical terms;
- attribution such as "有学者认为" or "裁判认为";
- judgment strength such as may, should, generally, necessarily, only, or in principle;
- conditions, exceptions, scope, causation, and sequence.

## Mandatory Rules

- Do not change the proposition supported by a footnote.
- Do not turn "some scholars argue" into the author's own assertion.
- Do not turn "may" into "must" or "inevitably."
- Do not turn an individual case into a general rule.
- Do not turn a material's conclusion into the author's conclusion.
- Do not add unconfirmed cases, norms, literature, facts, or substantive views.
- Do not remove conditions, exceptions, or scope qualifiers for fluency.
- Do not change causal direction.

## When A Footnote-Sensitive Sentence Needs Rewriting

Use one of these labels:

- `需人工复核：脚注支撑命题可能受表达调整影响。`
- `需人工复核：观点归属或判断强度发生变化风险。`
- `需人工复核：规范/案例/数据表述需要核对原文。`

Then explain the risk briefly.

## Safe Rewrite Moves

- Clarify sentence order without changing claim content.
- Split a long sentence while preserving qualifiers.
- Add a purpose sentence before material if it does not create a new factual claim.
- Make attribution explicit when it is already present.
- Convert an overbroad author sentence into a conditional sentence when the original support is limited.

## Unsafe Rewrite Moves

- Add a new authority because it seems relevant.
- Strengthen a claim to make it sound more decisive.
- Generalize from one case or article.
- Merge two differently supported claims under one footnote.
- Delete "possible," "in part," "under this condition," or similar limits.
